Likely Duplicates

—
Activities sharing a date with another whose distance and duration both fall within ±5%. Same-source re-syncs (Strava re-export of a Garmin ride) usually match exactly. Use Exclude to keep a duplicate file but stop it counting in stats; Delete archives the GPX to your backups folder. Tick the boxes on the rows you want to remove and hit Delete selected to clear them in one go.

Missing Activity Type

—
Activities with neither an explicit type nor a matched-region default — they fall into "Unknown" and skew per-type rollups. Click a type chip to assign one inline, or Open the ride to set a more specific title / region first.

Data Issues

—
Activities flagged for things like impossibly fast top speeds, GPS teleports, jitter that inflated the recorded distance, or implausible climb gradients. GPS Spikes have their own tab and are excluded from this list. Use Approve to silence a ride's flags, or Open in Logs to inspect and fix it (Trim, Smooth GPS, exclude, etc.).

GPS Speed Spikes

—
Rides with bursts of GPS noise that produced impossibly high speeds — usually a Strava downsample artefact, occasionally a real signal-loss-and-recover. The raw speed chart reveals the spikes; the repaired chart shows what the recorded max would be after fixing them. Hover either chart to pin the matching point on the map. Repair clamps the bad legs to local-median distance. Approve as-is leaves the data alone and stops flagging.

Odd Times

—
Activities whose start time (in the ride's local timezone) falls between 21:00 and 07:00. Either a genuine night ride or a timestamp that didn't get correctly localized at parse time. Use −1h / +1h to shift the activity's clock when the timezone is off, or Approve to accept the time as recorded.
